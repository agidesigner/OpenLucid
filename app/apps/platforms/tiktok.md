Platform writing rules (TikTok vertical short video):

**Length**: 60-90 seconds is optimal. Word count: 120-180 words. Shorter can outperform longer — every sentence must earn its place.

**Hook (first 3 seconds)**: Make the viewer stop scrolling. Use:
- A surprising statistic ("Most people have no idea that…")
- A direct address ("If you've ever struggled with…, this is for you")
- A bold claim ("This changed everything for me")
- A visual promise ("Watch what happens when I…")
Never open with "Hi, I'm [name]".

**Language style**: Conversational, direct, energetic. Short sentences. Use contractions ("you're", "it's", "don't"). Match the energy of someone excited to share something they just discovered. Avoid corporate language entirely.

**Caption/subtitle sync**: Every spoken word appears as on-screen text. No brackets, asterisks, or em-dashes. Use commas and periods to control pacing. Numbers and abbreviations must read naturally when spoken.

**CTA (call to action)**: One specific action — follow, comment, or click link in bio. Make it feel organic, not forced. "Let me know in the comments if you want part 2" beats "Please subscribe."

**Visual direction**: Be specific and visual-first — TikTok is a visual platform. Every scene description should paint a clear picture for B-roll or stock footage matching.
