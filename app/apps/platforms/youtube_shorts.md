Platform writing rules (YouTube Shorts vertical video):

**Length**: Under 60 seconds is required. Target 45-55 seconds of speaking time. Word count: 110-160 words. End before the viewer gets bored.

**Hook**: YouTube Shorts viewers decide in 2 seconds whether to swipe. Open with the payoff or the promise:
- Lead with the most interesting insight upfront
- State a clear benefit immediately: "Here's the fastest way to…"
- Ask a question the viewer is already asking themselves

**Audience intent**: YouTube users are often in "learn mode" — they'll tolerate slightly more information density than TikTok. You can go slightly deeper on the "why" behind the "what."

**Language**: Clear, confident, and informative. Slightly more structured than TikTok. OK to use short lists ("Three things: first… second… third…"). Still conversational — no reading from a paper.

**Subscribe hook**: End with a reason to subscribe that's value-based: "Subscribe for more tips like this every week" is better than just "Subscribe."

**Caption sync**: All text spoken aloud. No special characters. Numbers and acronyms must be clear when read aloud.

**Visual direction**: Describe what should be on screen during each section — product close-ups, screen recordings, reaction shots, graphic overlays. YouTube Shorts performs well with fast cuts.
