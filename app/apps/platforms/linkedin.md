Platform writing rules (LinkedIn post / article):

**Audience mindset**: Professionals reading between meetings. They want insight, not fluff. They'll share content that makes them look smart to their network.

**Post types** (pick one deliberately):
- **Harsh-truth one-liner**: 25-50 words, payoff IS the hook. Hormozi-style: "If you have no money, you should have no shame." Can hit 10K+ likes on strength of the line alone.
- **Numbered promise**: "5 mistakes I made at $1M ARR. Here's #1:" / "7 things that build a personal brand." The number-plus-promise hook carries its own gravity.
- **Contrast list**: "What LinkedIn gurus say / What actually works" — two-column comparison. Dominant archetype (~25% of top posts).
- **Lesson post**: "I thought X. I was wrong. Here's what I learned."
- **Framework post**: A named model (3-step, 5-pillar) they can save and reuse.
- **Data story**: Specific numbers + what they mean for the reader's work.
- **Contrarian take**: Challenge accepted wisdom with evidence.
- **Behind-the-scenes**: Real process, real numbers, real people — not polished case studies.

**Opening line (the "hook line")**: This is ALL that appears in the feed before "see more" (~140-210 chars on mobile). Best hooks are 6-8 words. Must make them click.
- Great: "We lost $400K on our first enterprise deal. Here's what I'd do differently."
- Great: "Most PMs measure the wrong thing."
- Great: "Here's a harsh truth. Nobody is coming to save you."
- Weak: "Excited to share my thoughts on..."
- Weak: "In today's fast-paced world..."

**Re-hook at line 2-3**: The biggest mid-post dropoff is at the "see more" cut. Put a tease in line 2 or 3 — "But here's what surprised me…" / "And here's what's wild…" — to justify the click. Appears in ~40% of top long posts.

**Structure**:
- Short paragraphs (1-3 lines). LinkedIn's reading environment is hostile to walls of text.
- Use line breaks generously — whitespace increases dwell time.
- Numbered points or dashes for scannability; contrast lists for comparisons.
- **For narrative posts**: save the payoff for the end.
- **For one-liner / contrarian posts**: the hook IS the payoff — don't bury it.

**Style**:
- First-person ("I", "we") — professional but personal
- Specific > general. Name the company, the year, the exact number.
- No empty buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "disrupt" as filler). High-intensity voice (Hormozi-style discipline/grind) is fine if it's earned by substance.
- No motivational quotes intro, no "grind" talk without concrete backing.

**Emoji**: Sparingly and purposefully. `👇` before a list, `✌️` or `🔥` as sign-off, `🔔` for follow-cue. Under 3 per post. No decorative sprinkling.

**Hashtags**: 0-3 max, or skip entirely. 2024-2026 algorithm research shows hashtags provide no reach boost and >5 actively suppresses reach. Most top performers (Hormozi, Bloom, many Welsh posts) use zero.

**Length**:
- **Short post** (150-300 words / ~900-1,800 chars): default. Median top-post length.
- **Ultra-short one-liner** (25-50 words): valid high-performing format for harsh-truth / contrarian voice.
- **Article** (1,000+ words): deep framework, multi-chapter narrative — lower reach but builds authority.

**CTA** — three viable patterns (pick one):
- **Specific question**: "What's your take on X?" — avoid lazy "Agree?" or "Thoughts?"
- **Comment-for-lead-magnet**: "Comment GUIDE and I'll DM you the playbook." Now a dominant CTA pattern; ~8× reach of external links.
- **Low-friction reaction**: "Drop a 🔥 if this hits." Works for punchy one-liner posts.

**Optional P.S. / P.P.S.**: End with P.S. for a secondary ask (newsletter, product plug, follow) separated from the main CTA. ~30% of top posts use this.

**Output**: In sections, use `text` for narrative flow. The opening section must have a killer first line that works standalone in the feed. Close with the chosen CTA (not a generic one).
