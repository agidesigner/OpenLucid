Platform writing rules (LinkedIn post / article):

**Audience mindset**: Professionals reading between meetings. They want insight, not fluff. They'll share content that makes them look smart to their network.

**Post types** (pick one deliberately):
- **Lesson post**: "I thought X. I was wrong. Here's what I learned."
- **Framework post**: A named model (3-step, 5-pillar) they can save and reuse
- **Data story**: Specific numbers + what they mean for the reader's work
- **Contrarian take**: Challenge accepted wisdom with evidence
- **Behind-the-scenes**: Real process, real numbers, real people — not polished case studies

**Opening line (the "hook line")**: This is ALL that appears in the feed before "see more". Must make them click.
- Great: "We lost $400K on our first enterprise deal. Here's what I'd do differently."
- Great: "Most PMs measure the wrong thing."
- Weak: "Excited to share my thoughts on..."
- Weak: "In today's fast-paced world..."

**Structure**:
- Short paragraphs (1-3 lines). LinkedIn's reading environment is hostile to walls of text.
- Use line breaks generously — whitespace increases dwell time.
- Numbered points or dashes for scannability.
- Save the payoff for the end but keep them engaged throughout.

**Style**:
- First-person ("I", "we") — professional but personal
- Specific > general. Name the company, the year, the exact number.
- No buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "disrupt" — all dead).
- No motivational quotes, no "grind" talk.

**Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant ones at the end. Don't sprinkle throughout.

**CTA**: LinkedIn rewards engagement. End with a specific question that invites comments ("What's your take on X?" works; "Agree?" doesn't).

**When to use article vs post format**:
- **Post** (short, 1300 chars max): Quick insight, single idea — higher reach
- **Article** (long-form): Deep framework, multi-chapter narrative — lower reach but builds authority

**Output**: In sections, use `text` for narrative flow. The opening section must have a killer first line. Close with a conversational question.
